<p><ul><li>Stereolithogrphy Apparatus (SLA)</li><li>Laminated Object Manufacturing (LOM)</li><li>Selective Laser Sintering (SLS)</li><li>Three Dimension Printing (3DP)</li><li>Fused Depostion Modeling (FDM)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Rapid prototyping in the automotive market involves various additive manufacturing technologies that expedite product development. Stereolithography (SLA) uses a laser to cure resin into solid parts, enabling high precision. Laminated Object Manufacturing (LOM) builds models by layering sheets of material, ideal for larger prototypes. Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) fuses powdered materials using lasers, suitable for functional testing. Three-Dimensional Printing (3DP) applies binder to powders, creating detailed parts. Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) extrudes thermoplastic filaments, popular for durable prototypes and tooling.</p></p>
